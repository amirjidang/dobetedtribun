Chris Daughtry's Life Has Changed Forever Following His Stepdaughter's Tragic Passing

Posted by Tandra Barner on Sunday, August 18, 2024

  • Is The Band Daughtry Still Together?

Chris Daughtry's life took a heartbreaking turn in 2021 after he suffered the significant loss of his mother and his stepdaughter. The tragedy of his stepdaughter led to the postponement of the band Daughtry’s upcoming shows. Several months after, the former American Idol contestant shared in ‘The Kelly Clarkson Show’ how he felt losing two of the important persons in his life in a year, and his realizations about not taking for granted the time spent with family and loved ones.

Despite this loss, Chris Daughtry and his wife Deanna, have shown remarkable resilience and strength as a family to honor the memory of their late daughter. Chris Daughtry has continued to pursue his passion for music and performing. The drastic changes in his life and career had led the musician to make a difference for a cause.

What Is Chris Daughtry Doing Now?

Chris Daughtry founded his record label, Dogtree Records back in 2013. The label was inspired by 19 Recordings and RCA Records, and its purpose is to provide a platform for upcoming artists to showcase their talent and reach a wider audience. Under the Dogtree Records banner, Chris has worked with several talented musicians, helping to bring their music to the forefront of popular culture. The label is known for its commitment to promoting new and innovative artists, and for its dedication to helping them grow their careers.

Chris Daughtry and the rest of his band members released their first studio album under the Dogtree record label, ‘Dearly Beloved’ which was released in 2021 and was the debut album to be released under that imprint, with no major record labels involved. The two singles from that album, ‘World On Fire’ and ‘Heavy Is The Crown’ proved its success on the music charts with their powerful, melodic, and dynamic production. Chris Daughtry described their album, ‘Dearly Beloved’ as 100 percent therapy. He described the freedom he feels with this album, and what will separate this one from the rest.

Daughtry is invigorated and seeking new and exciting sounds. He is also incorporating his passion for acting, action, and comic books into his work. Essentially, he is putting just as much, if not more, of himself into his work as he has in the past. Dogtree Labels have released eight records since 2021, all under the artistry of the rock band Daughtry.

Is The Band Daughtry Still Together?

The rock band Daughtry is still active and together. They continue to perform live shows and tour around the world. Daughtry performed on July 23, 2022, at the McGrath Amphitheater in Cedar Rapids. The band has never let more than three years pass between albums. Chris Daughtry stated that this is his passion, and he is not thinking of making music as a work, but rather as a labor of love.

The earnest, urgent, and often poignant tunes of the band have connected with a legion of fans around the world. Chris Daughtry and the rest of his band members have to adapt, however, during the pandemic that has affected their touring grind. But Daughtry persevered to have an enviable career filled with highlights and has been back on the road again. The Grammy-nominated American rock band also brought the sound of their ‘Dearly Beloved Tour’ to the Samala Showroom at the Chumash Casino Resort last November.

The album went on to sell more than 6 million copies in the U.S. and went six times Platinum. Four songs from the album enjoyed top 20 Billboard Hot 100 success and regular radio play. ‘Dearly Beloved’ is the sixth studio album released by Daughtry, but it is also the first studio album they released under a new record label. Daughtry is currently touring across one country and has eight upcoming shows. Their next tour date is at Nick Rayns LCR, UEA in Norwich, and the O2 Academy in Bristol. The rest of their scheduled shows for 2023 is available on their website.

How Has Music Helped Chris Daughtry Heal?

Music played a significant role in the healing process of Chris Daughtry who has lost someone that he dearly cherished. Music gave him the power to evoke emotions and memories and brought comfort and solace in difficult times.

Back in September 2022, when Grammy-winning singer, songwriter, producer, and best-selling author Richard Marx released his album 'Shame On You', Chris Daughtry was credited with co-writing the rock section of the album, specifically, the track ‘One More Yesterday’.

Apart from songwriting and compositions, Chris Daughtry grabs every chance he has to perform for a live audience. The rock star and Halestorm singer Lzzy Hale took the stage on January 10 at The Basement East in Nashville, with an Alice in Chains tribute band, and performed a powerful rendition of ‘Man in the Box’. They also performed their version of 'Rain When I Die' featuring Lzzy and her Halestorm bandmate, Joe Hottinger. Lzzy Hale and Chris Daughtry also collaborated on a cover of Journey’s ‘Separate Ways’ in celebration of the song’s 40th anniversary.

What Are The Charity Works And Causes Supported By Chris Daughtry And His Band?

Chris Daughtry has been active in charitable works throughout his career. He is known for his involvement with several organizations and causes that are close to his heart and has used his platform to raise awareness about important issues.

Chris Daughtry is involved with St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, a leading pediatric treatment and research facility that is focused on finding cures for childhood cancers. He has performed at several events to raise money, donated autographed guitars as hourly incentives for this cause, and has been an outspoken advocate for the work of St. Jude.

Chris Daughtry and his band have been supporting numerous charities since they started their career including disaster relief efforts and environmental protection initiatives. Some of these charitable organizations are: Autism Speaks, Love Our Children USA, Malaria No More, MusiCares, Music Empowers Foundation, Musicians on Call, ONE Campaign, Parkinson Society Maritime Region, Save the Children, Save The Music Foundation, The Salvation Army, and UNICEF.

Chris Daughtry's life may not be as exciting and glamorous compared to the rock star that he is, but he became resilient, found his sense of purpose through his music, and cherishes the meaningful relationships he has with his family and close friends.
