Netflix Teen Drama Grand Army Is Cancelled After One Season, And Fans Are Upset

Posted by Billy Koelling on Tuesday, August 20, 2024

Earlier this week, Variety reported that the Netflix teen drama series Grand Army will be canceled, after just one season.

Grand Army, which was originally based on Katie Cappiello’s play called Slut, centered around five students attending the largest public high school in Brooklyn as they fight to combat racial discrimination, economic differences, and sexual identities, while also trying to make a name for themselves in the world.

The coming-of-age series starred Odessa A’zion (Joey Del Marco), Odley Jean (Dominique Pierre), Amir Bageria (Siddhartha Pakam), Maliq Johnson (Jayson Jackson), Amalia Yoo (Leila Kwan Zimmer), and many others.

RELATED: Everything We Know About The Cast Of Netflix's 'Grand Army'

Cappiello created the show, and served as executive producer, along with Joshua Donen, Beau Willimon, Jordan Tappis, Nicolette Donen, and Elizabeth Kling.

Many fans were shocked to hear that the teen drama series will come to an end after just one season. When the series premiered on the streaming platform last October, it received mostly positive reviews, earning a rating of 71 percent on Rotten Tomatoes and 7.6 stars on IMDB.

Although Netflix has not released an official statement that explains the reason behind Grand Army's cancellation, it’s speculated that the view counts were simply not high enough to justify the show's budget.
