A Peek Inside Scooter Braun's Luxurious $65 Million Brentwood Mansion

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 10, 2024

Quick Links

  • New Digs
  • Inside Braun’s New Brentwood Mansion
  • Additional Amenities
  • Up Next

It’s been a year of ups and downs for music manager Scooter Braun. He shot to stardom after discovering a 12-year-old YouTube sensation, superstar Justin Bieber and today, Braun represents the likes of Ariana Grande, Cody Simpson, Carly Rae Jepsen and others.

In July 2021, Braun filed for divorce from his wife of seven years, Yael Cohen. The two have three children together and seemingly enjoyed the game of house flipping in the past decade.

In 2014, they purchased a $13.1 million mansion in Brentwood, CA, and Cohen’s parents moved in across the street in 2018. Also in 2014, Braun put his former bachelor pad on the market for $4.6 million and listed a second Los Angeles home for $11.6 million.

In 2020, the couple purchased the mansion next door to their Brentwood home, sold by John Travolta and his late wife Kelly Preston for a whopping $18 million.

In addition, the former couple previously owned a home in Montecito, CA, featured in a 2018 issue of Architectural Digest. They paid $10.9 million and in light of the divorce filings, sold it in Sept. for $28 million.

Now that Braun is out on his own and with a net worth of $400 million according to Celebrity Net Worth, he’s still breaking records and making noise among the Hollywood elite.

New Digs

Architectural Digest reports Braun has purchased a $65 million mansion in Brentwood, breaking a record for the priciest residential deal in Brentwood to date and second largest in Los Angeles’ Westside real estate market.

The modern farmhouse is 19,000 square feet and was owned by Larry and Kathleen Paul, co-owners of the NFL’s Pittsburgh Steelers. Architect Noah Walker designed the ultra-modern home and Jamie Bush served as interior designer.

With six bedrooms and nine and a half bathrooms, the home sits on four acres and boasts 13-foot ceilings and beautifully landscaped designs by Christine London.

Inside Braun’s New Brentwood Mansion

The contemporary home includes a bedroom suite in its own wing of the home, to ensure ultimate privacy. It has dual Poliform walk-in closets and a sitting area separating the bedroom by a double-sided fireplace.

According to Fancy Pants Homes, sliding doors open to balconies overlooking a pristine backyard and includes views of the ocean. The master bath is just as luxurious as the bedroom itself and has dual, large vanities, a freestanding tub, a chaise lounge for getting ready to go out for the evening and an attached outdoor seating area.

Amenities throughout the home include a walk up a beautiful spiral staircase to an upstairs home office with views of the ocean, a main level home office with attached outdoor seating and private entrance for those high-profile clients.

When Braun is ready to entertain, he can invite friends over for a movie in the 12-seat home theater, complete with acoustic wall paneling and commercial cinema quality Sony projection system.

The large family area offers comfortable seating, a pool table, and floor to ceiling windows overlooking the outdoor pool. Other rooms in the mansion include a bar and Vegas-style card playing table, a 1,650-bottle wine cellar, indoor pool and gym with steam room.

Throughout the home, Danish oak ceilings and a natural color palette gives visitors a vibe of simple, yet modern and classy.

Additional Amenities

A separate guest house on the property allows Braun to welcome famous friends for an extended stay. The home includes a living room, dining room, bedroom, bathroom, kitchen and washer/dryer.

A pool house is available for those long summer nights and outdoor pool parties.

The outdoor seating area also boasts a large fire pit – an amenity Braun has previously said he can’t live without, given the number of songs and artists he’s discovered while seated around one.

Up Next

The sky is the limit for the ultimate businessman, despite a few setbacks this year.

Staying in Brentwood is surely important to Braun to be near his three young children, as the former couple plan to co-parent and the music mogul is well-established in the area.

He owns record label Schoolboy Records, maintains his talent management company with well-known stars and launched a holding company, Ithaca Holdings, which owns portions of television, film and technology industries over the past several years. With a reported iron-clad prenup, even those investments owned by both Cohen and Braun are sure to pay off.

While Braun continues to work through divorce proceedings and settles into his new Brentwood mansion, the drive and passion he shows for the music industry is sure to shine through.
