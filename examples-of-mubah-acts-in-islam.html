What things are Makruh in Islam?

Makruh food, determined by the Quran, states that man should only eat pure food, and anything that is impure is regarded as makruh. This includes food that is spoiled or rotten. Into this comes now prawn, shrimp, crabs—all of which are carrion eaters.

What are mustahab acts?

Mustahabb (Arabic: مُسْتَحَبّ‎, lit. ‘beloved thing’) is an Islamic term referring to recommended, favoured or virtuous actions. Mustahabb actions are those whose ruling (ahkam) in Islamic law falls between mubah (neutral; neither encouraged nor discouraged) and wajib (compulsory).
