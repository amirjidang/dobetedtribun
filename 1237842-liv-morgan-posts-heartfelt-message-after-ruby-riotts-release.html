Liv Morgan Posts Heartfelt Message After Ruby Riotts Release

Posted by Tandra Barner on Saturday, August 17, 2024

The Riott Squad is no more following WWE’s release of Ruby Riott earlier this morning. This leaves Liv Morgan alone on WWE SmackDown without her best friend at her side.

This morning’s WWE releases were shocking and caught everyone off guard; the names were shocking. Ruby Riott being among them.

Shortly after WWE announced Ruby Riott’s release, Liv Morgan took to social media to post a heartfelt message regarding her friend and former tag team partner.

“Whoever is lucky enough to get Heidi Lovelace on their roster, you win,” Morgan tweeted.

Whoever is lucky enough to get Heidi Lovelace on their roster, you win.

— LIV Morgan (@YaOnlyLivvOnce) June 2, 2021

For those unfamiliar, Heidi Lovelace was Ruby Riott’s name on the independent wrestling scene prior to being signed by WWE. It’s a safe bet that she will return to her former name after her 90-day no-compete with WWE expires.

It’s worth noting that Aleister Black, who was among the releases this morning, has already changed his name back to Tommy End on his Twitter account, so Ruby Riott might not be too far behind. As of this writing, Riott has yet to comment on her WWE release.

Ruby Riott signed with WWE back in December of 2016. She had a good run on the black and gold brand of NXT before being called up to the WWE main roster alongside Liv Morgan and Sarah Logan as the Riott Squad for the SmackDown brand. This immediately drew comparisons to Paige’s Absolution group on WWE RAW that formed just days before.

Although coming close a few times during her five-year run, Ruby Riott never captured any championships during her time with WWE.

There is no doubt that Ruby Riott will be heavily coveted from multiple national promotions when her 90-day deal is up. Between AEW, IMPACT Wrestling, and Ring of Honor, you can rest assured that Riott will land on her feet quickly.
