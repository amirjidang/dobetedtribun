Tate Mcrae Ethnicity: Exploring the Multicultural Heritage!!

Tate McRae was born on July 1, 2003, in Calgary, Canada. Her dad is from Canada and has Scottish roots, and her mom is from Germany, making Tate of mixed ethnicity.

When Tate was only 4, her family moved to Oman, and she lived there for three years. During that time, her mom taught her how to dance, and she went to The American International School Muscat.

Later, she attended Western Canada High School, but because she had a busy schedule, she switched to online school.

Tracing the Inspiring Journey, Milestones, and Unstoppable Ascendancy in the Dazzling Career of Tate Mcrae!!

Tate McRae started dancing when she was just 4, learning from her mom. At 6, she began recreational training and got more serious at 8, competing with Drewitz Dance Productions. 

By 11, she mastered all dance styles at YYC Dance Project, owned by her mom. She also trained in ballet at the School of Alberta Ballet.

In 2013, McRae began her career as a voice actress for Lelaloopsy. Her dance career took off, making her a finalist at the New York City Dance Alliance’s 2014 National Gala and winning Best Female Dancer at the 2015 Dance Awards.

She danced in Walk Off the Earth’s “Rule The World” video and on the Ellen DeGeneres Show in 2016. McRae performed at the 2016 Teen Choice Awards and again on Ellen in October 2016. In 2017, she danced to Demi Lovato‘s “Tell Me You Love Me” and appeared in Nothing More’s “Just Say When” video.

Inspired by Dua Lipa and Post Malone, and dancers like Justin Timberlake and Britney Spears, McRae signed with RCA Records in 2019. Her EP “All The Things I Never Said” dropped in January 2020 with singles like “You Broke Me First” and “Vicious.” 

The success led to a deal with Sony/ATV. She was named an artist to watch in 2021 by Amazon Music, Pandora, The Independent, Uproxx, and NME.

In March 2021, McRae released “Too Young To Be Sad,” featuring songs like “Bad Ones” and “Slower.” Her single “Feel Like Shit” in November 2021 is a lead for her debut studio album in 2022. Tate McRae has collaborated with Lil Mosey, Ali Gatie, Troye Sivan, Jeremy Zucker, and more.
