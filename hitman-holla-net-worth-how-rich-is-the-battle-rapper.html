Early Life of Hitman Holla

Gerald Fulton, Jr., better known as (Hitman Holla), is an American battle rapper, professional athlete, and actor best known for his work as a battle rapper in the SMACK/URL rap circuit and as a recurring cast member of the MTV, VH1, and MTV2 improv comedy show Wild ‘N Out since Season 6.

Hitman Holla was born on March 29, 1988, in St. Louis, Missouri, USA. Gerald Fulton Senior and Sue Fulton, both African-Americans, gave birth to him. Showout, Holla’s younger brother, is also a fighting rapper. One of Showout’s rap battle videos went viral on WorldStarHipHop.

Hitman earned a scholarship to attend California State University, Northridge. He used to be more interested in athletics. He is a major basketball fan. In reality, he played basketball while attending college.

Rapping has become more interesting to him than athletics as he has matured. He was inspired by listening to X-Factor and Mook songs. Holla is heavily influenced by their gritty vocal style and incredible lyrics.

Hitman Holla Net Worth and Battle Rapping Career

He made his debut on the MTV show Wild ‘N Out,’ where he was the second rap battle artist to compete. He rose to prominence as a result of his singularity. In 2017, he collaborated with a new rapper called Jeff and released his new album, “Diddy Dum Dum.”

He recently appeared in the film She Ball, and his rap battles on YouTube have received over 46 million views, which is a record. His face value has increased significantly in recent years. His brother establishes himself as a rapper. His name had previously been used in two murder cases, causing uproar. When his mother was ill, 50 Cent, the legendary rapper, assisted Hitman.

Hitman began his rapping career by participating in the Flight Klub league. He took part in his first rap fight with Remy D. Holla gradually improved his rapping skills and gained widespread acclaim after defeating Bill Collector. He then went on to play in rap battle leagues such as Battle America and SMACK/URL.

Throughout his rap battle career, Hitman Holla has faced a slew of rappers as opponents. He has fought against Cassidy, Hollow Da Don, Math Hoffa, Goodz, Arsonal, and Conceited, among others. He has seen both success and defeat when competing in rap battles.

Holla was barred from playing in SMACK/URL after threatening to beat one of the band’s members, Norbes. Following that, he appeared in the sixth season of the hit show Wild ‘n Out. He was the show’s second fighting rapper, following Conceited.

On June 11, 2013, Hitman released his first single, “Ball Game.” The album is available for $8.91 on Amazon. In 2016, he released the single “Diddy Dum Dum,” which featured rapper Jeff. Aside from music, he has appeared in the 2020 film “She Ball.”
