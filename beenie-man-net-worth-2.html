Early Life

Beenie Man, whose real name is Moses Anthony Davis, was born on August 22, 1973, in Kingston, Jamaica. He showed an interest in music from a young age and began toasting at the age of 5. His musical aspirations were encouraged by his uncle Sydney Knowles, who was a drummer for Jimmy Cliff. Beenie Man won the 1981 Tastee Talent contest and gained popularity through radio DJs. He released his debut album, “The Invincible Beany Man – The 10 Year Old D.J. Wonder,” in 1983.

“Music has always been my passion, and from a young age, I knew that it was what I wanted to do.” – Beenie Man

Career

After releasing his debut album, Beenie Man took a break from his music career to finish school and travel to the U.S., U.K., and Canada. He gained more popularity after performing at the 1992 Reggae Sunsplash festival.

Throughout his career, Beenie Man has released 18 studio albums, with several reaching the top of the Billboard Top Reggae Albums chart. His albums showcase his versatility and talent, with each release showcasing his growth as an artist.

“My music is a reflection of my experiences and the vibrant culture of Jamaica. I strive to connect with my audience and deliver music that resonates with them.”

Some of Beenie Man’s most popular hits include “Who Am I (Sim Simma),” “King of the Dancehall,” and “Girls.” These songs have become anthems in the dancehall genre and have garnered millions of streams and views.

Beenie Man has collaborated with numerous artists throughout his career. He has worked with icons like Janet Jackson, Akon, and Rihanna, showcasing his ability to cross genres and collaborate with artists from different backgrounds.
