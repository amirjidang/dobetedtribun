Here's What Jessica Szohr Has Been Up To Since 'Gossip Girl'

Posted by Kelle Repass on Friday, August 30, 2024

As far as teen dramas go, The CW’s 2007 series Gossip Girl is now a classic (although the reboot was poorly received years later). Not to mention, the show introduced fans to several breakout stars. Of course, these include the likes of Blake Lively, Leighton Meester, Penn Badgley, Ed Westwick, and Chace Crawford. One cannot forget the appearance of Michelle Trachtenberg as Serena’s [Lively] frenemy Georgina.RELATED: The Truth About Leighton Meester's Net Worth Since 'Gossip Girl'At the same time, the show featured an impressive supporting cast that includes Jessica Szohr. On the show, the actress played Vanessa Abrams. The character was first introduced during the first season, although Vanessa didn’t really appear much in the later seasons. That said, the actress did make an appearance during the series finale. Since then, Szohr has taken on several other notable roles and projects.

Jessica Szohr Famously Starred In This Music Video

Back in 2013, Szohr starred Taylor Swift’s music video for her hit song 22. As fans may know, Szohr has been a longtime Swiftie. And so, the actress was than happy to join the video. “Taylor wrote a song and all of her girlfriends . . . we’ve all been through breakups and stuff together,” Szohr told Us Weekly. “So, she asked a few of us to come and have a fun day at the beach.”

And while there was some work involved, the Gossip Girl star didn’t really mind. “It was great. We were just in Malibu, and we got to eat pizza all day and then dance around and jump on the trampoline,” Szohr recalled. “It was just a fun, free Saturday.”

At the same time, Szohr kept busy with several film projects. For starters, there’s the Shawn Levy comedy The Internship, which is headlined by Owen Wilson, Rose Byrne, Josh Gad, and Vince Vaughn who co-wrote the film.

Soon after, the actress joined the cast of the romantic comedy Brightest Star, the cast of which also includes Christopher Lowell, Allison Janney, and Clark Gregg. Szohr soon followed this up with the horror-thriller House at the End of the Street and crime thriller 10 Cent Pistol. Later on, the actress joined the cast of the Mark Wahlberg comedy Ted 2.

Later On, Jessica Szohr Returned To TV Too

Having started in television, it seems Szohr continues to have a soft spot for episodic work. For starters, she joined the cast of the USA Network medical drama Complications. At first, however, the actress was hesitant to take on the role of nurse Gretchen Polk.

“I was like, ‘Oh, a medical show. I don’t know.’ Not that medical shows aren’t great-they are excellent; Grey’s Anatomy and ER were wonderful. But I questioned if I wanted to play a nurse for potentially five or six years.” the actress recalled during an interview with Shock Ya!. “The pilot was wonderful though, so I booked it.”

Unfortunately, Complications was canceled after just one season. Nonetheless, Szohr remained busy, starring in the sport drama Kingdom and the Emmy-nominated series Twin Peaks. Szohr had a recurring role in the Emmy-winning dramedy Shameless.

Later on, the actress joined the cast of Seth MacFarlane’s adventure comedy The Orville. Here, she plays Xelayan Lieutenant Talla Keyali. It was exactly the role that McFarlane approached her about from the beginning.

“I sat down with Seth maybe around January [of 2018], and he told me what he wanted to do with Talla….,” Szohr told TV Line. “The social issues that he touches on with this show are so amazing and genius, and it’s so fun because we get to play with it, in space.”

On the show, Szohr got to have a little reunion with former Gossip Girl co-star Meester. “I love that girl so much, and I was super pumped when Seth told me she was coming,” the actress remarked. “That episode actually is one of my favorites of the season.”

Jessica Szohr Has Been Hosting A ‘Gossip Girl’ Podcast

The Orville may have been keeping Szohr quite busy these days, but that doesn’t mean she doesn’t have time for other projects, especially when its Gossip Girl-related. Recently, the actress has been hosting the Gossip Girl podcast XOXO.

“I'm trying to say, ‘Hey, come with me, listen to some good music. Let's talk about the fashion. Let's talk about why it worked, why we had fun.’ I think we need more of that right now,” Szohr told Teen Vogue of her new podcast. “The irony is it's a Gossip Girl podcast, but I'm not here to gossip about things. We touch on challenges, but it's a place for people to be able to come and listen, laugh, and get lost.”

Meanwhile, fans can look forward to seeing Szohr in the upcoming film All-Star Weekend that marks Jamie Foxx’s directorial debut. Foxx stars in the movie alongside Gerard Butler, Benicio Del Toro, Robert Downey Jr., Jeremy Piven, and Snoop Dogg.
