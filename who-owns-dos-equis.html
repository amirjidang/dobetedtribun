Who Owns Dos Equis? Dos Equis Company History

Posted by Patria Henriques on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

Dos Equis, the popular Mexican beer brand, has a fascinating history that dates back to its founding in 1897 by Wilhelm Hasse, a German immigrant. The brand’s name, Dos Equis, meaning “two Xs” in Spanish, symbolizes the Roman numeral “XX” on its logo. Over the years, Dos Equis has seen changes in ownership due to mergers and acquisitions.

Currently, the brand is owned by the esteemed Dutch brewing company, Heineken International. Heineken acquired Dos Equis when they purchased the Mexican brewing company Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma in 2010, thereby becoming the custodian of the Dos Equis brand.

Dos Equis is renowned for its two flagship beers, Dos Equis Lager Especial and Dos Equis Ambar, which are beloved for their distinct Mexican-style lagers.

A Brief History of Dos Equis

Dos Equis, founded in 1897 by Wilhelm Hasse, is a renowned Mexican beer brand with a fascinating history. Hasse, a German brewer, established the Moctezuma Brewery in Veracruz, Mexico, and began producing a Vienna-style lager called “Siglo XX” to commemorate the approaching 20th century. Later, the beer was rebranded as Dos Equis, which means “two Xs” in Spanish, symbolizing the Roman numeral “XX”.

Throughout the years, Dos Equis has gained significant popularity and global recognition as one of Mexico’s finest beer brands. Its ownership has undergone several changes due to mergers and acquisitions, ultimately falling under the helm of Heineken International in 2010.

Today, Dos Equis is celebrated for its exceptional Mexican-style lagers and remains an enduring symbol of quality and craftsmanship in the beer industry.

The Ownership Journey of Dos Equis

The history of Dos Equis ownership has been marked by significant changes over time. In 1985, Heineken, the Dutch brewing company, acquired a minority stake in the Moctezuma Brewery, the parent company of Dos Equis. Gradually, Heineken increased its share, eventually leading to the full acquisition in 2010. This acquisition also encompassed the Mexican beer operations of FEMSA, the former parent company of Moctezuma Brewery.

As a result, Dos Equis came under the ownership of Heineken International. Today, Dos Equis is managed and operated by the Heineken Group, which is globally recognized as one of the largest and most prominent beer brands.

Dos Equis and Fomento Economico Mexicano (FEMSA)

Before being acquired by Heineken, Dos Equis was part of Fomento Economico Mexicano (FEMSA), a Mexican multinational beverage and retail corporation. FEMSA played a significant role in the Mexican brewing industry and was involved in various acquisitions and partnerships.

In 1985, Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma, the parent company of Dos Equis, merged with Cervecería Cuauhtémoc to create a combined brewing operation. This merger led to the formation of Cervecería Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma, which was responsible for producing and distributing Dos Equis until the acquisition by Heineken in 2010.

This collaboration with FEMSA allowed Dos Equis to benefit from the expertise and resources of a leading Mexican beverage and retail company. The partnership strengthened Dos Equis’ position in the market and contributed to its growth and success.

Dos Equis Founder Wilhelm Hasse

Wilhelm Hasse, a German immigrant, is the visionary founder behind the iconic Mexican beer brand, Dos Equis. Born in 1846 in Lübbenau, Germany, Hasse embarked on a journey to Mexico in the early 1890s, driven by his passion for brewing excellence.

Recognizing the vast potential for German brewing techniques to thrive in the Mexican market, Hasse established the Moctezuma Brewery in Monterrey. This marked the beginning of his extraordinary journey in creating a beer brand that would capture the hearts of beer enthusiasts around the world.

Hasse’s expertise and dedication to crafting high-quality brews led to the birth of Dos Equis in 1897. With meticulous attention to detail and a commitment to brewing traditions, he laid the foundation for the brand’s success, setting it apart from the competition.

The legacy of Wilhelm Hasse lives on through the continued popularity and acclaim of Dos Equis, which has become synonymous with exceptional Mexican beer. His pioneering spirit and unwavering pursuit of brewing perfection have solidified Dos Equis as a revered brand in the industry.

Dos Equis Brewery Operations

Dos Equis beer is crafted at several brewing plants operated by Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma Brewery, a subsidiary of Heineken International. These state-of-the-art facilities are strategically located in Monterrey, Guadalajara, Toluca, Tecate, Orizaba, Navojoa, and Meoqui.

At these brewing plants, Dos Equis is produced alongside other renowned beer brands, including Sol, Bohemia, Superior, Carta Blanca, Noche Buena, Indio, Casta, and Tecate.

With an impressive annual production of 3.09 gigaliters, equivalent to 26.33 million U.S. beer barrels, Dos Equis exemplifies its commitment to delivering exceptional beers to consumers worldwide.

The Dos Equis brewery’s dedication to quality brewing ensures that every sip of Dos Equis beer is a testament to its rich heritage and outstanding craftsmanship.
