Early Life and Career

Rakim, born William Michael Griffin Jr., was raised in Wyandanch, New York. From a young age, Rakim showed a passion for music and began rapping as a child. His talent caught the attention of Eric Barrier, and the duo Eric B & Rakim was formed. They quickly gained recognition in the hip-hop community for their innovative style and thought-provoking lyrics.

Their debut album, “Paid in Full,” released in 1987, became an instant classic and solidified their status as one of the most influential acts in hip-hop. The album showcased Rakim’s smooth flow and complex rhyme schemes, setting a new standard for lyricism. Their subsequent albums, such as “Follow the Leader” and “Let the Rhythm Hit ‘Em,” further established their reputation as pioneers of the genre.

After releasing four successful albums together, Eric B & Rakim split in 1992, with Rakim pursuing a solo career. He continued to release critically acclaimed albums, including “The 18th Letter” and “The Master,” further showcasing his lyrical prowess and cementing his legacy as one of the greatest MCs in rap history.
