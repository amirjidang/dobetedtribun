The Maroon 5 Sneakerhead: The 8 Most Expensive Sneakers In Adam Levine's Collection

Adam Noah Levine is a famous American singer who is the lead vocalist of the popular pop-rock band Maroon 5. The established musical sensation is the recipient of a multi-platinum album, three Grammy Awards, and three American Music Awards, making him one of the most accomplished artists in the music industry. The multi-talented personality has flawlessly diversified his career path through his contribution to television, with his role as a coach on NBC's hit reality talent show The Voice exemplifying his creative mindset and passion towards his craft.

Adam's successful stint as a musician has earned him a an enormous fortune and fan-following, with his extravagant lifestyle detailing his love for sneakers. The American musical artist is the proud owner of some of the world's most exclusive and luxurious pairs of sneakers ever made, with his awe-inspiring collection only getting more extensive and more valuable with time.

Nike Air Yeezy II (Red October)

The Nike Air Yeezy range of sneakers encompasses a strong desirability factor that very few shoemakers can match up to. Sneakerheads across the globe have high regard for the decorated 'Yeezy' moniker, and Adam Levine is no exception.

The revered musician's enormous collection of kicks features the uber-stylish Nike Air Yeezy II' Red October' edition from the immensely diverse and colorful Yeezy range. This swanky pair of kicks features a midfoot strap and a variety of aesthetically pleasing design elements that take their inspiration from the notable 'Nike Tech Challenge Line' range of sneakers.

Although retailing for $250 upon their launch in 2014, these stand-out sneakers command an eye-watering market value north of a staggering $19,000 in recent times.

Air Jordan 5

The revered Air Jordan trademark has developed some of the most eye-catchy and immensely functional pairs of kicks over the years, with their Air Jordan 1, Air Jordan 3 'White Cement' and even the Air Jordan 4 'Bred' remaining some of the most valuable and highly desirable renditions of the 'Air Jordan' moniker.

The Air Jordan range of sneakers possess their own story of conception that makes them all the more appealing, and the Air Jordan 5 is easily one of the brand's most enthralling examples of an inspirational outlook.

The uber-stylish kicks take their inspiration from a World War II fighter plane and feature an array of aesthetically pleasing design elements in the form of a 3M reflective tongue, mesh netting, and a flame on the midsole that gives them an invigorating presence.

With an asking price north of $6,000 at auction in recent times, Adam Levine's awe-inspiring collection only seems to get more exciting.

Air Jordan 3 'Black Cement'

This iteration of the Air Jordan range of sneakers encompasses a decorated past. The 'Black Cement' version of the Air Jordan was originally launched to the public in 1988, with its stellar updates over the years, making it a popular and immensely desirable pick amongst sneakerheads across the globe.

The uber-stylish kicks are, in essence, a black and gray version of Michael Jordan's 'Third Signature' shoe and feature a tasteful mix of leather, elephant print, and visible air.

With an asking price north of $600 in recent times, this 2018 launch from the Air Jordan stable is on a steady ascent in value and only adds to the extravagance of Adam Levine's enormous collection.

Air Jordan 4 'Friends and Family Purple'

The story behind the development of these uber-stylish kicks makes them all the more special. Based on Michael Jordan's fourth signature sneaker, this revamped version of the iconic Air Jordan was the brainchild of critically-acclaimed rapper Travis Scott.

This exclusive rendition of the Air Jordan 4 features an upper derived from suede and canvas that gives it a robust dynamic, while the bright crimson detailing helps elevate its aesthetic appeal that much more.

With an eye-watering valuation north of $30,000 in recent times, this unique rendition of the Air Jordan 4 is easily one of the more expensive examples of Adam Levine's mesmerizing sneaker collection.

Air Jordan 1 Travis Scott

The Air Jordan 1 takes the baton of one of the most fabulous pairs of sneakers in existence. These legendary pair of kicks are often credited to kickstarting the enormous success of the 'Air Jordan' moniker, with the pair's over three-decade-long presence in the segment translating to an enormous market value.

The intricately designed and immensely comfortable Air Jordan 1 is considered a must-have for pure sneakerheads across the globe, with its premium construction using top-notch quality materials like leather and its unmatched exclusivity, making it all the more special.

Its decorated history coupled with the ever-rising popularity of the 'Air Jordan' moniker makes it one of the most expensive examples in Adam Levine's mesmerizing collection, with pristine examples of the famous kicks selling upwards of an eye-watering $550,000 at auction.

Air Jordan 5 Retro T23' Tokyo'

The story behind the inception of these elegant pairs of kicks and their premium construction justifies their exorbitant price tag. Jordan released these uber-stylish kicks to the public to celebrate the opening of Japan's 'Tokyo 23' store, with its overall construction consisting of several tasteful design elements.

This rendition of the coveted Air Jordan range of sneakers features a suede-derived upper finished in Varsity Maize, a neutral Wolf Gray color scheme on the tongue, and even mesh inserts that go well with the overall design language of the fashionable piece of footwear.

With an asking price north of $4,000 at auction, the 'Tokyo 23' is yet another fancy addition to Adam Levine's contemporary sneaker collection.

Air Jordan 4 Retro 'Eminem x Carhartt’: $26,000

This customized rendition of the Air Jordan 4 details sophistication. The overall design of these uber-stylish pair of kicks includes a black Carhartt twill upper, metallic silver lace tabs, a visible air unit, and even a contrasting white midsole that elevates the style quotient for maximum engagement.

Additionally, the customized Air Jordans feature signature detailing in the form of Eminem's backward 'E' logo and a Jason Voorhees mask at the heel, making it one of the most unique and unorthodox examples of a bespoke pair of kicks.

With an asking price north of a whopping $120,000 at auction, these one-of-kind Air Jordan 4s exude flamboyance and make camp at Adam Levine's elegant sneaker collection.

Travis Scott x Air Jordan 6

Travis Scott's collaboration with the Jordan brand has yielded fruitful results in recent times. The revered musical artist has depicted his creative mindset through his one-of-kind sneaker designs, with every new release from this unique merger outshining the previous.

This customized iteration of the Air Jordan 6 features an ensemble of tasteful design elements like a glow-in-the-dark sole, perforated underlays with a reflective design, and even several shades of British khaki that work in tandem to help it stand out from the competition.

The Cactus Jack branding present on its lace lock and right heel combines with a smiley face graphic on the medial midsole, while the infrared accents and Nike Air branding on its left heel pay homage to the original Air Jordan 6.

Overall, these customized pairs of kicks detail elegance and are all set to skyrocket in value in the near future.

Adam Levine is undeniably one of the most successful musical artists of the modern era. The pop-rock vocalist has cemented his entertainment industry legacy with authority and amassed a generous fortune in the process. Several celebrities possess a unique area of interest or hobby that details their extravagant lifestyle, and Adam Levine is no exception. His rather unorthodox inclination towards purchasing some of the world's most exclusive and exorbitant sneakers details passion, with his mesmerizing collection only getting more diverse and contemporary with each passing moment.
